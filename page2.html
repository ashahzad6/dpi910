Project 2 

 This is a group project. Group sizes range from one to three students. If there are four of you, break up into two groups.
For your project, you must demonstrate that you are able to perform a thorough evaluation of the security of one or more real world web applications using the tools and techniques we have studied in this course.

You may choose one of the following:

bug bounty of a real web site (preferred)
a real open source web application
a custom project of your own that you must clear with your professor (example: one student performed a security assessment of his employer's web site, with his employer's permission)

You must use the tools, techniques and methodologies studied in this course. You may also use others that you have found useful.
It is preferable that you choose a bug bounty for your project.
Ideas about how to pursue and complete your project can be found in the Project Resources section of the Project module of the Blackboard site for this course.
The following assumes you are choosing a bug bounty. If not, adjust your project accordingly. (e.g., a description of the open source application, rather than the bug bounty program)
A good source for understanding how to do a bug bounty is the following book:
Real-World Bug Hunting
A Field Guide to Web Hacking
by Peter Yaworski
Published by No Starch Press, July 2019, 264 pp.
ISBN-13: 978-1-59327-861-8
https://nostarch.com/bughunting
You should read at least the following chapters:

Introduction
Ch 1: Bug Bounty Basics
CH 19: Finding Your Own Bug Bounties
Ch 20: Vulnerability Reports
Appendix A: Tools
Appendix B: Resources

The other chapters are very useful.
Your deliverable for this project is a professionally written report outlining:

all group members full names
an introduction summarizing your project
a thorough description of the bug bounty program, including a thorough description of the scope of the program
a thorough description of the app(s) being tested
a thorough description of methodologies you used to complete your project
vulnerability reports as described in Chapter 20 of the book mentioned above
a summary of your work (more thorough than the intro)

Your project will be marked according to thoroughness of execution, and accompanying analysis, rather than results. Of course, results are encouraged!
You must demonstrate a thorough understanding of the material covered in this course.
You are not required to submit a bug bounty report to the target if you do not want to, unless it is a condition of participating in the bug bounty program.
NOTE: Your target must have an appreciable degree of complexity, otherwise there is insufficient challenge to earn a grade. Please use your discretion when choosing a project. If it lacks complexity, you will not receive full marks. Choosing a target that is too easy may lead to failure.
